Ayrton Senna da Silva

"I am not designed to come second or third. I am designed to win."

Ayrton Senna was not only one of the best F1 racing drivers of all time, but also a philantropist.

Senna's won 3 F1 driver championships with McLaren-Honda. In 1994 he switched to Williams, having died on the fourth GP of that season at Imola (San Marino).

Website

Considered by many fellow drivers as a model of competitiveness and ruthlessness inside the cockpit, outside of it he was an incredibly kind human being, helping many people (especially his brazillian compatriots) overcoming their needs.

Senna had a tough rivalry with his fellow brazillian racing driver Nelson Piquet, but arguably his main rival was the french Alain Prost, with whom he had the fiercest rivalry ever known to the F1 world.

Toleman

Senna drove for the uncompetitive Toleman in his freshman year (1984). His most memorable performance was in very rainy Monaco, where he almost won!

Lotus

At Lotus Senna experienced both his first pole position and his first win at Portugal, in 1985. Ayrton drove for Lotus until 1987.

McLaren

Senna drove for McLaren from 1988 until 1993. He won all of his 3 championships there, where he also forged the greatest rivalry ever of F1 with Alain Prost.

Williams

Senna drove for Williams in 1994 for only 4 races. He died on his fourth race at San Marino due to a mechanical failure.
